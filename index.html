LoLTrack

Web Scraper & Structuring Data

Personal project of mine that I started after taking my first introductory CS course at Berkeley (CS61A).

I learned to use packages such as BeautifulSoup to scrape data off of the web and implemented data structures I learned in my first CS class to organize and store data.

LoLTrack pulls data from your League of Legends games, courtesy of op.gg, and compares your performance on a champion to that of the average player. LoLTrack also stores your perfomance data and allows you to compare your current perfomance on a champion compared to before.

Gitlet

Version-control System

Made by a group of five for one of the projects assigned in my second CS course at Berkeley (CS61B: Data Structures and Programming Methodology).

Basic mini version of git, allows users to work in a version control system, includes basic methods such as branching, merging, pulling, etc.

Uses data structures learned in the class and includes use of outside packages not learned in the class for processes such as serializing objects and storing files into the local disk.
